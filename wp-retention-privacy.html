Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2026

Short version: WP Retention processes data only on your WordPress server. We do not collect, transmit, or store your subscribers' personal data on any external server. The plugin is designed from the ground up to keep all data local.

1. Who we are

WP Retention is a WordPress plugin published by WP Retention ("we", "us", "our"). This Privacy Policy explains how the WP Retention plugin and this website (wpretention.com) handle data.

This policy covers two distinct contexts: (1) the plugin itself, which is installed on your WordPress server, and (2) this marketing website, which you are currently visiting.

2. The plugin — data processed on your server

When WP Retention is installed and active on a WordPress site, it processes data locally on that WordPress installation. This data never leaves your server unless you explicitly export it.

Data collected per subscriber

  • Login timestamps — the date and time of each login event, stored in wp_usermeta
  • Content view events — records of protected content pages viewed, stored in the wpr_events table (post ID + timestamp, no content)
  • Payment failure events — records of subscription payment failures (subscription ID + timestamp)
  • Risk score — a calculated integer (0–100) stored in wp_usermeta
  • Cancellation data — if a subscriber initiates a cancel flow, their selected reason and whether they accepted an offer
  • Coupon codes — unique WooCommerce coupon codes generated for individual subscribers

This data is processed for the purpose of calculating churn risk and triggering retention actions. The legal basis for this processing is legitimate interest of the WordPress site operator in retaining active subscribers.

Data retention

Event data in the wpr_events table is used for scoring calculations. The plugin does not automatically purge old events — site operators should implement their own data retention policies consistent with their local regulations.

All plugin data is completely removed when the plugin is deleted from WordPress: the wpr_events table is dropped, all _wpr_* usermeta keys are deleted, and all plugin options are removed.

Third-party data sharing

WP Retention does not share subscriber data with any third party. The plugin makes no external API calls that transmit subscriber personal data. All processing is local to the WordPress installation.

If the site operator has configured WooCommerce with Stripe or another payment processor, those processors may independently process payment data — this is outside the scope of WP Retention and governed by their respective privacy policies.

3. Site operators' responsibilities (GDPR)

If you operate a WordPress site in the European Economic Area, or serve subscribers located in the EEA, you are the data controller under GDPR for subscriber data processed by this plugin. You are responsible for:

  • Informing your subscribers about data processing in your own Privacy Policy
  • Maintaining a valid legal basis for the processing (legitimate interest is typically appropriate for retention activities)
  • Responding to data subject rights requests (access, deletion, portability) — plugin data can be exported via the wpr_events table and removed via plugin uninstall
  • Ensuring your WordPress hosting provider is GDPR-compliant

WP Retention, as a plugin publisher, acts as a data processor only to the extent that we provide the software. We do not process subscriber personal data ourselves.

4. This website (wpretention.com)

This marketing website may collect the following data:

  • Analytics — we may use privacy-respecting analytics to understand page traffic (no personal identifiers, no cross-site tracking)
  • Purchase data — when you purchase a license, your payment and contact details are processed by Freemius, our payment processor. Freemius acts as a merchant of record. Their privacy policy applies: freemius.com/privacy
  • Support emails — if you contact us at cookservices84@gmail.com, we retain your email for the purpose of responding to your inquiry

5. Cookies

This website does not use tracking cookies or advertising cookies. Freemius's checkout page may set functional cookies necessary for the checkout process.

6. Freemius SDK

The WP Retention plugin includes the Freemius SDK, which is used for license management and plugin updates. When you activate your license, the Freemius SDK communicates with Freemius servers to validate the license. This may transmit:

  • Your WordPress site URL
  • Your license key
  • WordPress and PHP version numbers
  • The plugin version

This data is processed by Freemius under their privacy policy. No subscriber personal data is transmitted to Freemius. Refer to freemius.com/privacy for full details.

On first activation, Freemius will ask for your consent before enabling any optional data collection (analytics). You may decline without affecting plugin functionality.

7. Data subject rights

If you are a subscriber on a WordPress site running WP Retention and wish to exercise your data subject rights (access, deletion, portability), please contact the operator of that WordPress site directly — they are the data controller for your data.

If you are a customer who purchased a WP Retention license and wish to exercise your rights in relation to your purchase data, contact us at cookservices84@gmail.com or refer to Freemius's data subject rights process.
