How it works

Four steps.
Fully automatic.

01

Track signals

WP Retention silently monitors login frequency, content consumption, and payment events — all inside your WordPress install.

02

Score every subscriber

Every night, a risk score (0–100) is recalculated per subscriber based on weighted behavioral signals you control.

03

Trigger actions

When a score crosses your threshold: send a personal email, apply a loyalty coupon, or flag them in your dashboard. Before they cancel.

04

Intercept cancellations

If they do reach the cancel button, a smart flow asks why — and offers the right response based on their answer. Pause, discount, or confirm.

FAQ

Questions.

Does it work without MemberPress or WooCommerce Subscriptions?
No. WP Retention requires either MemberPress or WooCommerce Subscriptions (or both) to be active on your site. It hooks into their subscription events to track behavior and detect churn signals. Without one of these, there's nothing to track.
Does my subscriber data get sent anywhere?
Never. WP Retention is 100% WordPress-native. All scoring data, event logs, and subscriber profiles are stored in your own database — in wp_usermeta and a single custom table. Nothing leaves your server. No third-party SDK, no external API calls for data.
Will it slow down my site?
The daily scoring runs via WP-Cron in batches of 50 subscribers — not during user requests. The content tracking hook is lightweight and async. On a typical membership site, the performance impact is immeasurable.
What happens if I uninstall the plugin?
A clean uninstall removes everything: the custom events table, all wp_usermeta fields prefixed with _wpr_, and all plugin options. Your database is left exactly as it was before installation.
How is this different from Churn Solution or ChurnKey?
Those are SaaS products with WordPress connectors — your data flows through their servers, you pay monthly, and they only act when someone reaches the cancel button. WP Retention detects at-risk subscribers days or weeks before that — and acts automatically. No monthly fees. No external data processing.
Can I customize the scoring weights?
Yes. From the settings panel, you can adjust the weight of each scoring signal (login inactivity, content consumption, payment failures, subscription age) to match your site's specific dynamics. A content-heavy membership site might weight content inactivity higher; a SaaS-style site might prioritize payment signals.
